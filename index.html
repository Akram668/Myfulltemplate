Discover the Fascinating History of Amphora with Herakles and Busiris

This amphora is a two-handled vessel for storing and transporting oil and wine, featuring a rare scene of the Greek hero Herakles (Hercules) battling Busiris, a legendary king of Egypt. According to myth, Busiris sacrificed foreign visitors to protect his land from plague, but Herakles disguised himself to end this inhuman practice. Herakles kills Busiris and attacks two white-robed Egyptian priests. The composition is decorated with elaborate patterns on the priest's garments and the altar. The Swing Painter, an Athenian black-figure artist known for a distinctive, whimsical style, is attributed with creating this piece.
